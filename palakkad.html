Palakkad is one of the 14 districts of the Indian state of Kerala with many natural, heritage, cultural and leisure tourism attractions. This is the largest district with an area of 4,478 km2 and which shares it boundary with Thrissur, Malappuram and Tamil Nadu. The district Palakkad is known as the "the granary of Kerala" and "Rice bowl of Kerala". Palakkad is situated at the foot of the Western Ghats with a vast expanse of fertile plains interspersed with rivers, mountain streams, forest and hills. The Palakkad pass act as a corridor between Kerala and neighboring Tamil Nadu and played a major role in the trade contacts between east and west costs of peninsular India.
